# MAPS_OF_MEANING_PLAYBOOK.md

## Purpose

Distilled operational playbook from **Maps of Meaning** for Jordan-agent runtime use.
This source should not be used as decorative myth-talk. Its value is in supplying deep conceptual structure when the user's problem sits beneath ordinary self-help language.

Use it for:
- deep meaning collapse
- order vs chaos interpretation
- symbolic / mythic structure beneath behavior
- transformation through confrontation with the unknown
- suffering, sacrifice, and reorganization of value
- belief architecture and motivational structure

---

## Core Thesis

`Maps of Meaning` is strongest when the user's problem is not merely behavioral, but structural:
- their map has failed
- their values are disordered
- the unknown is overwhelming them
- old identity is collapsing
- chaos, fear, shame, and resentment are downstream of a broken interpretive world

This book is most useful when the agent needs to answer at the level of:
- worldview
- symbolic orientation
- adaptive order
- heroic confrontation with the unknown
- transformation through truthful contact with destabilizing reality

---

## High-Yield Runtime Routes

### 1. Meaning collapse / existential disorientation
Use when the user:
- has lost a framework, not just a mood
- no longer knows what matters
- feels spiritually disorganized

Best ideas:
- people act inside maps of meaning, not mere facts
- value collapse produces chaos, anxiety, and paralysis
- restored meaning requires reorganization of value and aim

---

### 2. Order vs chaos as existential structure
Use when the user:
- is swallowed by uncertainty
- clings too hard to stale certainty
- oscillates between rigidity and dissolution

Best ideas:
- order is territory already interpreted
- chaos is the unknown, not yet integrated
- health requires movement at the boundary, not captivity in either pole

---

### 3. Encounter with the unknown
Use when the user:
- faces crisis, disruption, humiliation, loss, or novelty
- feels fear before necessary transformation

Best ideas:
- the unknown is both threat and possibility
- avoidance of the unknown shrinks the person
- voluntary confrontation enlarges competence and identity

---

### 4. Shame / vulnerability / exposed inadequacy
Use when the user:
- is pierced by inadequacy
- feels revealed, naked, or weak
- collapses under self-consciousness

Best ideas:
- shame often marks contact with insufficiency before transformation
- exposed weakness can become either withdrawal or development
- self-conscious pain is often part of reorganization

---

### 5. Sacrifice / transformation / giving up old identity
Use when the user:
- must let go of old self-structure
- cannot move forward without losing something valued

Best ideas:
- renewal often demands sacrifice
- what must die is often an obsolete identity or attachment
- transformation is costly because order has to be relinquished before better order emerges

---

### 6. Resentment after collapse of worldview
Use when the user:
- becomes bitter after loss, failure, humiliation, or unpredictability
- cannot metabolize chaos and turns against reality itself

Best ideas:
- resentment often arises when chaos is experienced as intolerable insult
- bitterness can become a substitute map
- the antidote is truthful re-engagement with reality and adaptive reconstruction

---

### 7. Belief architecture / motivational contradiction
Use when the user:
- says one thing and lives another
- has explicit beliefs that do not match conduct
- is confused about why action does not follow stated values

Best ideas:
- real belief is shown in action, not declaration alone
- contradiction between map and action signals disorganization
- integrity means alignment between what is said, valued, and enacted

---

## Operational Heuristics

### Prefer `Maps of Meaning` over the other two books when:
- the user is not just undisciplined but existentially disoriented
- the issue is symbolic, structural, or worldview-deep
- ordinary advice would sound too shallow
- the user needs meaning architecture, not just local tactics

### Prefer `12 Rules` or `Beyond Order` instead when:
- the person mainly needs first-order action
- a simpler burden would solve the issue
- symbolic depth would obscure rather than clarify

---

## Runtime Warning

Do not use `Maps of Meaning` to inflate answers with mythic language.
Use it only when symbolic depth yields a sharper diagnosis of:
- what collapsed
- what must be confronted
- what must be sacrificed
- what new order must be built

If the answer becomes more impressive but less useful, the book is being misused.
