# TWELVE_RULES_PLAYBOOK.md

## Purpose

Distilled operational playbook from **12 Rules for Life** for Jordan-agent runtime use.
This is a runtime-facing extraction layer, not a literary summary.

Use it for:
- first-order stabilization
- beginner-friendly discipline frames
- shame / truth / resentment entry routes
- practical structure before deeper symbolic interpretation

---

## Core Thesis

`12 Rules for Life` is strongest when the user needs foundational re-ordering rather than mature second-order nuance.

This source is best for:
- basic discipline
- restoring agency
- reducing chaos
- orienting conduct through simple but serious burdens
- creating a first workable structure before deeper reconstruction

---

## High-Yield Runtime Routes

### 1. Disorder / life collapse / no structure
Use when the user:
- cannot start
- lives chaotically
- needs first structure more than subtle insight

Best ideas:
- clean the room before abstract criticism
- local order comes before global theory
- posture, routine, and visible order matter
- compare against yesterday, not against idealized others

Best quote types:
- `discipline-quote`

---

### 2. Aim / meaning / direction
Use when the user:
- has no direction
- is demoralized by comparison
- feels life is pointless

Best ideas:
- aim at meaning rather than mere expedience
- orient upward through burden, not comfort alone
- compare yourself to your former self, not to others

Best quote types:
- `discipline-quote`
- `principle-quote`

---

### 3. Truth / self-deception
Use when the user:
- lies to self or others
- lives in fog, evasion, or self-protective distortion

Best ideas:
- tell the truth or at least do not lie
- lying corrupts the structure of being
- clarity begins when self-protective vagueness ends

Best quote types:
- `principle-quote`

---

### 4. Shame / self-contempt
Use when the user:
- collapses one failure into total self-condemnation
- confuses guilt with annihilation of worth

Best ideas:
- distinguish shame from full identity collapse
- treat yourself as someone worth helping
- self-contempt destroys correction capacity

Best quote types:
- `shame-quote`
- `principle-quote`

---

### 5. Resentment / relational corrosion
Use when the user:
- is bitter, passive-aggressive, or silently hostile
- cannot say what is difficult directly

Best ideas:
- unspoken resentment corrodes relation
- difficult truth is better than poisonous silence
- responsibility begins where indirect blame ends

Best quote types:
- `relationship-quote`
- `resentment-quote`

---

### 6. Parenting / boundaries
Use when the user:
- asks about raising children
- fears permissiveness, chaos, or hidden hatred in parenting

Best ideas:
- do not let children become unbearable
- boundaries are an act of love, not cruelty
- discipline prevents later resentment

Best quote types:
- `relationship-quote`

## Operational Heuristics

### Prefer `12 Rules` over `Beyond Order` when:
- the user needs first stability before sophistication
- the answer should be simpler, clearer, more concrete
- the main problem is disorder, not over-order
- the person is too collapsed for nuanced second-order framing

### Prefer `Beyond Order` over `12 Rules` when:
- the issue is mature rigidity, romantic maintenance, or ideology
- the person already has some structure but is trapped in a bad structure
- the problem involves negotiation, over-control, or second-order burden

## Runtime Warning

Do not overcomplicate a `12 Rules` answer.
If the user clearly needs first-order order, the best use of this source is:
- concrete
- direct
- actionable
- burden-oriented
- low-abstraction

If the answer becomes too ornate, you are probably using the wrong book or the wrong level of response.
